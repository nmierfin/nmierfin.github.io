The Process

Why and How did I design my website the way it is. I have specific reasons brought upon my past-experience in Job Interviews, Design, and my own Sense of Artistic Style.

Home Page

Firstly, I chose this font Lexenda Deca, because I like to have fonts that aren't too straight, but represent that I can be proffesional and creative at the same time. That's how I felt when choosing Lexenda Deca as my font. The homepage consists of special-made containers by me, they have an image of me, title, sub-title, and paragraph. This is sort of an impromptu cover letter with a more natural condensed version of my story, so anyone can see who I am without having to read a cover letter.

Project Pages

I used interactive cards with images & dynamic gifs, links (when needed), and descriptions to give a potential employer a visual representation of my projects. When reading a resume it is very difficult to get a sense of what a project is or is involved, and using visual queues a person can get a sense of (especially in the case of GIS) "Oh that's what that is." instead of a quick glance over trying to read every word for word trying to understand what I'm talking about.

Skills Page

Using advice from recruiters who look at personal websites, they said never to lay all of your skills out, specifically your level of confidence, as that's the job of the recruiter to determine through seeing your projects. However, I wanted to use my skills to show something else. Not only can you see some of the major languages and concepts I've learned, you can see my progression over the years, and see just how long I've actually been programming for. Using 2011, using an old IDE, giving that sense that I've been around for a while, and I'm not some new kid off the block when it comes to computer science.

Contact Me

On this page I wanted to be a little more creative with the dynamic aspect, and used tooltips to create a little hover effect over my contact me links. This is to show the person looking at my website that I am also able to use some creative programming in my website.
